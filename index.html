About Me

I got into development through games. A lot of my early motivation came from wanting to build fun, memorable experiences for players, and that passion still shapes how I approach software today.

Over time, that path grew into web and full-stack development, where I've worked with startups in hands-on roles building products across the stack. I've created apps and games for desktop, mobile, web, and VR, and I enjoy figuring out how to turn ideas into polished, practical solutions people actually want to use.

I've worked both as part of larger teams and on solo projects, so I'm comfortable collaborating closely with others or taking ownership and driving things forward on my own. That range has helped me build strong technical instincts, communicate clearly, and stay focused on delivering work that is reliable, thoughtful, and ready for real-world use.

I also teach part-time at Fanshawe College, which keeps me sharp and connected to the fundamentals. Whether I'm building a product from scratch, improving an existing one, or helping shape a new idea, I bring a mix of creativity, technical depth, and confidence in getting good work shipped.
